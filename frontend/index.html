The Problem

AI is making life-changing decisions. Is yours fair?

Hiring, lending, healthcare, criminal justice — hidden bias in AI systems is causing real harm, today. There's no universal standard to prove otherwise.

Amazon

Scrapped a hiring AI that penalised women

Resumes containing the word "women's" (e.g. women's chess club) were systematically downranked — learned entirely from historical data, no explicit programming required.

Banks

Charging minorities higher rates for identical applications

Loan approval AIs have been found to charge higher interest rates or outright reject minority applicants — even when their financial profiles were identical to approved candidates.

Hidden

Bias buried deep in model mathematics

The bias is never programmed in — it's learned from historical data. Removing a sensitive column doesn't remove the information; it just hides it in proxy features like zip code or college name.

India

No AI Act. No enforcement. No standard.

Unlike the EU (which has the AI Act), India has no law yet requiring companies to prove their AI is fair. There's both a technical gap and a legal policy gap — FairTrust fills both.

How It Works

Six-layer pipeline.
One ethical score.

Upload your model predictions and dataset. FairTrust handles the rest — from bias detection to compliance certification.

STEP 01

Model Ingestion

Upload your dataset (CSV/JSON) and model predictions. Define sensitive attributes, target variable, and fairness priorities — no .pkl or .pth files needed.

STEP 02

Bias Detection

Seven fairness dimensions evaluated across four Swiss-cheese layers — from systemic disparate impact down to individual prediction fairness.

STEP 03

SHAP / LIME Explainability

Individual and group-level feature attribution. Proxy bias detection by combining SHAP importance with correlation to sensitive attributes. Makes invisible decisions visible.

STEP 04

Remediation Engine

Doesn't just find problems — prescribes fixes. Rebalancing suggestions for group disparities, proxy feature removal, recalibration steps, and fairness-aware thresholds.

STEP 05

Three Audience Reports

Developer gets technical SHAP analysis and risk flags. Regulator gets compliance checklist and ethical score. End-user gets plain-language explanation of what influenced their outcome.

STEP 06

JEDI Certification

Generates a machine-readable certificate — Fully Compliant, Conditional, High Risk, or Non-Compliant — that can be presented to regulators and clients as proof of ethical compliance.

Evaluation Dimensions

Seven angles of fairness

Each dimension is independently scored and weighted by user priority, then combined into a single ethical score from 0–100.

DIM 01

Individual Fairness

Similar individuals are treated similarly by the model, regardless of demographic group.

DIM 02

Group Fairness

Defined demographic groups receive equitable outcomes across the model's predictions.

DIM 03

Demographic Bias

Covers gender, age, race, disability, and socioeconomic bias across all protected attributes.

DIM 04

Calibration Bias

Model confidence is equally reliable across all demographic subgroups — 80% means 80%.

DIM 05

Disparate Impact

The 80% rule — approval rate of the minority group must be at least 80% of the majority rate.

DIM 06

Counterfactual Bias

Outcomes should not change if only a sensitive attribute is flipped — same person, different outcome is a red flag.

DIM 07

Intersectional Bias

Overlapping disadvantages across multiple identities (e.g. elderly + female + low-income) that compound and only appear at the intersection — invisible to single-dimension analysis.
