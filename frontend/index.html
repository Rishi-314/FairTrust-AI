The Problem

AI is making life-changing decisions. Is yours fair?

Hiring, lending, healthcare, criminal justice — hidden bias in AI systems is causing real harm, today. There's no universal standard to prove otherwise.

Amazon

Scrapped a hiring AI that penalised women

Resumes containing the word "women's" (e.g. women's chess club) were systematically downranked — learned entirely from historical data.

Banks

Charging minorities higher rates

Loan approval AIs charge higher interest rates or reject minority applicants — even with identical financial profiles.

Hidden

Bias buried deep in model mathematics

The bias is never programmed in — it's learned from historical data. Removing a sensitive column doesn't remove the information.

India

No AI Act. No enforcement. No standard.

Unlike the EU, India has no law requiring companies to prove their AI is fair. FairTrust fills both technical and policy gaps.

Evaluation Dimensions

Seven angles of fairness

Each dimension independently scored and weighted, combined into a single ethical score.

DIM 01

Individual Fairness

Similar individuals are treated similarly by the model, regardless of demographic group.

DIM 02

Group Fairness

Demographic groups receive equitable outcomes across the model's predictions.

DIM 03

Demographic Bias

Gender, age, race, disability, and socioeconomic bias across all protected attributes.

DIM 04

Calibration Bias

Model confidence equally reliable across all subgroups — 80% means 80% for everyone.

DIM 05

Disparate Impact

The 80% rule — minority approval rate must be at least 80% of the majority rate.

DIM 06

Counterfactual Bias

Outcomes shouldn't change if only a sensitive attribute is flipped.

DIM 07

Intersectional Bias

Overlapping disadvantages across multiple identities — invisible to single-dimension analysis.
